Biography

Rafael López García was born in Ferrol, A Coruña (Spain) on the 8th of December of 1982.

He obtained both his B.S. Degree in Computing Science and his Master's Degree in Information and Communication Technologies from the University of A Coruña and he is now a Ph.D. candidate at Kyoto University (in Japanese 京都大学). He is also a member of the Database Society of Japan (DBSJ, in Japanese 日本データベース学会).

His primary fields of interest in IT are Web Technologies, Mobile Applications and Information Retrieval. He is also interested in Education, and he aims to write his Ph.D. Thesis on improvement of IR systems for educational purposes. He expects to become a teacher at high educational levels (technical school or university) in the future.

Apart from these academic worlds, he is very interested in the language, history, culture and traditions of Japan.

CV

Academic background in Computing Science:

  • B.S. Degree in Computing Science from the University of A Coruña (UDC) in October 2006.
  • The final project "Design and implementation of an interactive service for IPTV", mentee by the late Associate Professor Ph.D. Víctor M. Gulías Fernández, received a qualification of "excellent". It consists in a J2EE Web Application especially oriented to Digital Interactive Television (although it also works on PC). It uses a VoDKA on-demand video service. The application itself is an online multiplayer strategy videogame.
  • Master's Degree (DEA) from the Department of Information and Communication Technologies (TIC) of the University of A Coruña in July 2009.
  • The Master's Thesis, mentee by Associate Professor Ph.D. Fernando Bellas Permuy, received a qualification of "excellent". It is a compilation of the research work developed in this 2-year period. This work focus on several topics: e-Commerce, Mashups, Internet Information Retrieval, etc.
  • B.S. Degree in System Engineering from the University of A Coruña (UDC) in March 2010.
  • The final project "Framework for Teaching and Research in Information Retrieval", mentee by Associate Professor Ph.D. Carlos Alberto Pan Bermúdez, received a qualification of "excellent". Described here.
  • Currently developing the Ph.D. Thesis at the Department of Social Informatics, Kyoto University (in Japanese 京都大学), concretely on Web Information Retrieval and Education areas, mentee by Professor Ph.D. Katsumi Tanaka (in Japanese 田中克己教授).
  • Participation in several research projects.
